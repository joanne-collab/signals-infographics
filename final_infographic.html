The Memory Consolidation Journey

Information is initially acquired and processed during wakefulness.

Slow-wave (NREM N3) and REM sleep cycles begin the integration process.

Memories are transferred from the hippocampus to the neocortex for permanent long-term storage.

Critical Pillars of Nocturnal Health

• The glymphatic system actively flushes neurotoxic waste, including amyloid-beta, during deep rest.

• Cytokine production peaks during sleep to bolster the immune response against infection.

• Sleep serves as a primary regulator for glucose metabolism and insulin sensitivity.
